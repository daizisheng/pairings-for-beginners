3.5 Definition: the Zariski topology

3.5 定义：Zariski拓扑

Proposition-Definition   The correspondence $V$ satisfies the following formal properties:

(i) $V(0) = \mathbb{A}^n_k$;   $V(A) = \varnothing$;

(ii) $I \subset J \implies V(I) \supset V(J)$;

(iii) $V(I_1 \cap I_2) = V(I_1) \cup V(I_2)$;

(iv) $V\!\bigl(\sum_{\lambda \in \Lambda} I_\lambda\bigr) = \bigcap_{\lambda \in \Lambda} V(I_\lambda)$.

Hence the algebraic subsets of $\mathbb{A}^n_k$ form the closed sets of a topology on $\mathbb{A}^n_k$, the Zariski topology.

命题-定义：对应 $V$ 满足以下性质：
(i) $V(0)=\mathbb{A}^n_k$，$V(A)=\varnothing$；
(ii) $I \subset J \Rightarrow V(I) \supset V(J)$；
(iii) $V(I_1 \cap I_2) = V(I_1) \cup V(I_2)$；
(iv) $V(\sum_\lambda I_\lambda) = \bigcap_\lambda V(I_\lambda)$。

因此 $\mathbb{A}^n_k$ 的代数子集构成 $\mathbb{A}^n_k$ 上一个拓扑的闭集，这个拓扑叫做 Zariski拓扑。

The above properties are quite trivial/ˈtrɪv.i.əl/ 平凡的，显然的, with the exception of the inclusion $\subset$ in (iii). For this, suppose $P \notin V(I_1) \cup V(I_2)$; then there exist $f \in I_1$, $g \in I_2$ such that $f(P) \neq 0$, $g(P) \neq 0$. So $fg \in I_1 \cap I_2$, but $fg(P) \neq 0$, and therefore $P \notin V(I_1 \cap I_2)$.   Q.E.D.

上面的性质大多显然，唯一需要说明的是(iii)中的 $\subset$ 方向。假设 $P \notin V(I_1) \cup V(I_2)$，则存在 $f \in I_1$、$g \in I_2$ 使得 $f(P) \neq 0$、$g(P) \neq 0$。于是 $fg \in I_1 \cap I_2$ 但 $fg(P) \neq 0$，所以 $P \notin V(I_1 \cap I_2)$。Q.E.D.

The Zariski topology on $\mathbb{A}^n_k$ induces a topology on any algebraic set $X \subset \mathbb{A}^n_k$: the closed subsets of $X$ are the algebraic subsets.

It's important to notice that the Zariski topology on a variety is very weak, and is quite different from the familiar topology of metric/ˈmet.rɪk/ 度量的 spaces like $\mathbb{R}^n$. As an example, a Zariski closed subset of $\mathbb{A}^1_k$ is either the whole of $\mathbb{A}^1_k$ or is finite; see Ex. 3.12 for a description of the Zariski topology on $\mathbb{A}^2_k$.

If $k = \mathbb{R}$ or $\mathbb{C}$ then Zariski closed sets are also closed for the ordinary topology, since polynomial functions are continuous/kənˈtɪn.ju.əs/ 连续的. In fact they're very special open or closed subsets: a nonempty Zariski open subset of $\mathbb{R}^n$ is the complement/ˈkɒm.plɪ.ment/ 补集 of a subvariety, so automatically dense/dens/ 稠密的 in $\mathbb{R}^n$.

The Zariski topology may cause trouble to some students; since it is only being used as a language, and has almost no content, the difficulty is likely to be psychological/ˌsaɪ.kəˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl/ 心理上的 rather than technical.

$\mathbb{A}^n_k$ 上的Zariski拓扑自然地在任何代数集 $X \subset \mathbb{A}^n_k$ 上诱导一个拓扑：$X$ 的闭集就是 $X$ 的代数子集。

重要的是要注意，簇上的Zariski拓扑非常弱，与 $\mathbb{R}^n$ 这样的度量空间的通常拓扑大不相同。例如，$\mathbb{A}^1_k$ 的Zariski闭集要么是整个 $\mathbb{A}^1_k$，要么是有限集。参见习题3.12对 $\mathbb{A}^2_k$ 上Zariski拓扑的描述。

如果 $k=\mathbb{R}$ 或 $\mathbb{C}$，Zariski闭集也是通常拓扑的闭集，因为多项式函数是连续的。实际上它们是非常特殊的开集或闭集：$\mathbb{R}^n$ 中非空的Zariski开集是某个子簇的补集，因此在 $\mathbb{R}^n$ 中自动稠密。

Zariski拓扑可能会给一些学生带来困扰；但它只是一种语言工具，几乎没有什么实质内容，困难更多是心理上的而非技术上的。

Footnote. $\mathbb{A}^n$ is thought of as a variety, whereas $k^n$ is just a point set. Think of this as pure pedantry/ˈped.ən.tri/ 学究气 if you like; compare (4.6) below, as well as (8.3).
