2.12 Inflexion, normal form

2.12 拐点；标准形式

Every cubic in $\mathbb{P}^2_{\mathbb{R}}$ or $\mathbb{P}^2_{\mathbb{C}}$ can be put in the normal form/ˈnɔːr.məl fɔːrm/ 标准形式

$$C : Y^2 Z = X^3 + aXZ^2 + bZ^3, \qquad (**)$$

or in the affine form

$$y^2 = x^3 + ax + b.$$
$\mathbb{P}^2_{\mathbb{R}}$ 或 $\mathbb{P}^2_{\mathbb{C}}$ 中的任意三次曲线都可以化为标准形式 $C: Y^2Z = X^3 + aXZ^2 + bZ^3$（记为 $(**)$），或者仿射形式 $y^2 = x^3 + ax + b$。

Now consider the above curve $C$; where does it meet the line at infinity $L : (Z = 0)$? That's easy, just substitute/ˈsʌb.stɪ.tjuːt/ 代入 $Z = 0$ in the defining polynomial $F = -Y^2Z + X^3 + aXZ^2 + bZ^3$ to get $F|_L = X^3$; this means that $F|_L$ has a triple zero at $P = (0, 1, 0)$. To see what this means geometrically/ˌdʒiː.əˈmet.rɪ.kəl.i/ 在几何上, set $Y = 1$, to get the equation in affine coordinates $(x, z)$ around $(0, 1, 0)$:

$$z = x^3 + axz^2 + bz^3.$$

This curve is approximated/əˈprɒk.sɪ.meɪ.tɪd/ 近似的 to a high degree of accuracy by $z = x^3$: the behaviour is described by saying that $C$ has an inflexion point at $(0, 1, 0)$.

现在考虑上面的曲线 $C$；它在哪里和无穷远直线 $L: (Z=0)$ 相交呢？很简单，把 $Z=0$ 代入定义多项式 $F = -Y^2Z + X^3 + aXZ^2 + bZ^3$ 就得到 $F|_L = X^3$；这说明 $F|_L$ 在 $P = (0,1,0)$ 有一个三重零点。为了看出这在几何上意味着什么，令 $Y=1$，得到 $(0,1,0)$ 附近的仿射坐标 $(x,z)$ 下的方程：$z = x^3 + axz^2 + bz^3$。这条曲线可以非常精确地用 $z = x^3$ 来近似：这种行为就是说 $C$ 在 $(0,1,0)$ 处有一个拐点。

More generally, an inflexion/ɪnˈflek.ʃən/ 拐点 point $P$ on a curve $C$ is defined by the condition that there is a line $L \subset \mathbb{P}^2_k$ such that $F|_L$ has a zero of multiplicity/ˌmʌl.tɪˈplɪs.ɪ.ti/ 重数 $\geq 3$ at $P$ (see Ex. 2.9; in fact necessarily $L = T_P C$ by (2.8, b), and the multiplicity $= 3$ by (1.9)). It is not hard to interpret this in terms of the derivatives/dɪˈrɪv.ə.tɪvz/ 导数 and second derivatives of the defining equations: for example, if the defining equation is $y = f(x)$, then the condition for an inflexion point is simply $\frac{d^2 f}{dx^2}(P) = 0$; this corresponds in the diagram to the curve passing through a transition from being 'concave downwards' to being 'concave upwards'. There is a general criterion/kraɪˈtɪr.i.ən/ 判据，标准 for a plane curve to have an inflexion point in terms of the Hessian, see for example [Fulton, p. 116] or Ex. 7.3, (iii).

更一般地，曲线 $C$ 上的拐点 $P$ 定义为：存在直线 $L \subset \mathbb{P}^2_k$ 使得 $F|_L$ 在 $P$ 处有重数 $\geq 3$ 的零点（见习题2.9；实际上由(2.8, b)，$L$ 必然就是切线 $T_P C$，由(1.9)重数恰好等于3）。用导数和二阶导数来理解这个条件并不难：例如，若定义方程为 $y = f(x)$，则拐点的条件就是 $\frac{d^2 f}{dx^2}(P) = 0$；在图形上对应的就是曲线从"下凸"过渡到"上凸"的转折点。判断平面曲线拐点有一个用Hessian矩阵的通用判据，参见[Fulton, p. 116]或习题7.3 (iii)。

It can be shown (see Ex. 2.10) that conversely, if a plane cubic $C$ has an inflexion point, then its equation can be put in normal form $(**)$ as above.

可以证明（见习题2.10），反过来，如果平面三次曲线 $C$ 有一个拐点，那么它的方程就可以化为上面的标准形式 $(**)$。
