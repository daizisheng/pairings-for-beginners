8.12 Affine Varieties and Schemes

仿射簇与概形

The coordinate ring $k[V]$ of an affine algebraic variety $V$ over an algebraically closed field $k$ (Definition 4.1) satisfies two conditions: (i) it is a finitely generated $k$-algebra; and (ii) it is an integral domain/ˈɪn.tɪ.ɡrəl dəˈmeɪn/ 整环. A ring satisfying these two conditions is obviously of the form $k[V]$ for some variety $V$, and is called a geometric ring (or geometric $k$-algebra).

代数闭域 $k$ 上的仿射代数簇 $V$ 的坐标环 $k[V]$（定义 4.1）满足两个条件：(i) 它是一个有限生成的 $k$-代数；(ii) 它是一个整环。满足这两个条件的环显然具有 $k[V]$ 的形式（对应某个簇 $V$），称为几何环（或几何 $k$-代数）。

There are two key theoretical results in Part II; one of these is Theorem 4.4, which states precisely that $V \mapsto k[V] = A$ is an equivalence/ɪˈkwɪv.əl.əns/ 等价 of categories between affine algebraic varieties and the opposite of the category of geometric $k$-algebras (although I censored out all mention of categories as unsuitable for younger readers). The other is the Nullstellensatz (3.10), that prime ideals of $k[V]$ are in bijection/baɪˈdʒek.ʃən/ 双射 with irreducible subvarieties of $V$; the points of $V$ are in bijection with maximal ideals.

第二部分有两个关键的理论结果；其一是定理 4.4，它精确地表述为 $V \mapsto k[V] = A$ 是仿射代数簇的范畴与几何 $k$-代数的对偶范畴之间的范畴等价（虽然我把所有关于范畴的提法都删掉了，因为不适合低年级读者）。另一个是零点定理 (3.10)，即 $k[V]$ 的素理想与 $V$ 的不可约子簇之间有双射；$V$ 的点与极大理想之间有双射。

Taken together, these results identify affine varieties $V$ with the affine schemes corresponding to geometric rings (compare also Definition 4.6).

综合起来，这些结果将仿射簇 $V$ 与对应于几何环的仿射概形等同起来（另见定义 4.6）。

The prime spectrum/ˈspek.trəm/ 谱 $\operatorname{Spec} A$ is defined for an arbitrary ring (commutative with a 1) as the set of prime ideals of $A$. It has a Zariski topology and a structure sheaf; this is the affine scheme corresponding to $A$ (for details see [Mumford, Introduction, or Hartshorne, Ch. II]). There are several quite distinct ways in which affine schemes are more general than affine varieties; each of these is important, and I run through them briefly in (8.14).

素谱 $\operatorname{Spec} A$ 对任意环（含幺交换环）定义为 $A$ 的素理想的集合。它具有Zariski拓扑和结构层；这就是对应于 $A$ 的仿射概形（详见 [Mumford, Introduction] 或 [Hartshorne, Ch. II]）。仿射概形在若干截然不同的方面比仿射簇更一般；每一个方面都很重要，我在 (8.14) 中简要讨论它们。

It's important to understand that for a geometric ring $A = k[V]$, the prime spectrum $\operatorname{Spec} A$ contains exactly the same information as the variety $V$, and no more. The NSS tells us there's a plentiful supply of maximal ideals ($\mathfrak{m}_v$ for points $v \in V$), and every other prime $P$ of $A$ is the intersection of maximal ideals over the points of an irreducible subvariety $Y \subset V$:

$$P = I(Y) = \bigcap_{v \in Y} \mathfrak{m}_v;$$

It's useful and (roughly speaking, at least) permissible/pəˈmɪs.ɪ.bəl/ 容许的 to ignore the distinction between varieties and schemes, writing $V = \operatorname{Spec} A$, $v$ for $\mathfrak{m}_v$, and imagining the prime $P = I(Y)$ ('generic point') as a kind of laundry mark stitched everywhere dense into the fabric of the subvariety $Y$.

重要的是要理解，对于几何环 $A = k[V]$，素谱 $\operatorname{Spec} A$ 所包含的信息与簇 $V$ 完全相同，不多也不少。零点定理告诉我们有充足的极大理想（点 $v \in V$ 对应 $\mathfrak{m}_v$），而 $A$ 的每个其他素理想 $P$ 是不可约子簇 $Y \subset V$ 上各点的极大理想的交： $$P = I(Y) = \bigcap_{v \in Y} \mathfrak{m}_v;$$ 忽略簇与概形之间的区别是有用的，且（大致说来至少）是容许的——写 $V = \operatorname{Spec} A$，用 $v$ 表示 $\mathfrak{m}_v$，并将素理想 $P = I(Y)$（"一般点"）想象为缝在子簇 $Y$ 的织物上的、处处稠密的一种标记。
