8.5 The Big Bang

大爆炸

History did not end in the early 1970s, nor has algebraic geometry been less subject to swings of fashion since then. During the 1970s, although a few big schools had their own special interests (Mumford and compactification/kəmˌpæk.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən/ 紧化 of moduli spaces, Griffiths' schools of Hodge theory and algebraic curves, Deligne and 'weights' in the cohomology/ˌkəʊ.həˈmɒl.ə.dʒi/ 上同调 of varieties, Shafarevich and K3 surfaces, Iitaka and his followers in the classification of higher dimensional varieties, and so on), it seems to me we all basically believed we were studying the same subject, and that algebraic geometry remained a monolithic/ˌmɒn.əˈlɪθ.ɪk/ 整体划一的 block (and was in fact colonising/ˈkɒl.ə.naɪ.zɪŋ/ 殖民的，占领的 adjacent areas of math). Perhaps the presence of just one or two experts who could handle the whole range of the subject made this possible.

历史并没有在1970年代初终结，此后代数几何也并非没有经历时尚的摇摆。在1970年代，虽然几个大的学派各有自己的特殊兴趣（Mumford与模空间的紧化、Griffiths学派的Hodge理论和代数曲线、Deligne与簇的上同调中的"权"、Shafarevich与K3曲面、飯高与他的追随者们在高维簇分类方面的工作，等等），但在我看来我们基本上都相信自己在研究同一门学科，代数几何仍然是一个整体的板块（实际上还在殖民数学的邻近领域）。也许正是因为有一两位能够驾驭整个学科范围的专家的存在，才使这成为可能。

By the mid-1980s, this had changed, and algebraic geometry seems at present to be split up into a dozen or more schools having quite limited interaction: curves and Abelian varieties, algebraic surfaces and Donaldson theory, 3-folds and classification in higher dimensions, K theory and algebraic cycles, intersection theory and enumerative/ɪˈnjuː.mər.ə.tɪv/ 计数的 geometry, general cohomology theories, Hodge theory, characteristic $p$, arithmetic/əˈrɪθ.mə.tɪk/ 算术的 algebraic geometry, singularity theory, differential equations of math physics, string theory, applications of computer algebra, etc.

到了1980年代中期，情况已经改变，代数几何目前似乎已分裂为十几个甚至更多相互交流相当有限的学派：曲线与Abel簇、代数曲面与Donaldson理论、三维簇与高维分类、K理论与代数闭链、相交理论与计数几何、一般上同调理论、Hodge理论、特征 $p$、算术代数几何、奇点理论、数学物理中的微分方程、弦理论、计算机代数的应用，等等。
