8.16 Acknowledgements and Name Dropping

致谢与提及的名字

It would be futile/ˈfjuː.taɪl/ 徒劳的 to try to list all the mathematicians who have contributed to my education. I owe a great debt to both my formal supervisors/ˈsuː.pə.vaɪ.zəz/ 导师 Pierre Deligne and Peter Swinnerton-Dyer (before he became a successful politician and media personality); I probably learned most from the books of David Mumford, and my understanding (such as it is) of the Grothendieck legacy derives largely from Mumford and Deligne. My view of the world, both as a mathematician and as a human being, has been strongly influenced by Andrei Tyurin.

试图列出所有对我的教育有所贡献的数学家是徒劳的。我对我的两位正式导师Pierre Deligne和Peter Swinnerton-Dyer（在他成为成功的政治家和媒体人物之前）深表感激；我可能从David Mumford的书中学到了最多，我对Grothendieck遗产的理解（就我所理解的而言）主要来自Mumford和Deligne。我对世界的看法，无论作为数学家还是作为人，都深受Andrei Tyurin的影响。

My approach to what an undergraduate algebraic geometry course should be is partly based on a course designed around 1970 by Peter Swinnerton-Dyer for the Cambridge tripos/ˈtraɪ.pɒs/ (剑桥大学)荣誉学位考试, and taught in subsequent years by him and Barry Tennison; my book is in some ways a direct descendant/dɪˈsen.dənt/ 后继的 of this, and some of the exercises have been taken over verbatim/vɜːˈbeɪ.tɪm/ 逐字地 from Tennison's example sheets. However, I have benefitted enormously from the freedom allowed under the Warwick course structure, especially the philosophy of teaching (explicitly stated by Christopher Zeeman) that research experience must serve as one's main guideline/ˈɡaɪd.laɪn/ 指导方针 in deciding how and what to teach.

我对本科代数几何课程应该是什么样的看法，部分基于Peter Swinnerton-Dyer约在1970年为剑桥荣誉学位考试设计的课程，此后由他和Barry Tennison在接下来的几年讲授；我的书在某种意义上是它的直接后继，一些习题是从Tennison的例题单中逐字搬来的。然而，我从华威大学课程体制所允许的自由中获益巨大，特别是这一教学理念（由Christopher Zeeman明确提出）：研究经验必须作为决定如何教学和教什么的主要指导方针。

The 'winking torus' appearing in (2.14--) comes to me from Jim Eells, who informs me he learnt it from H. Hopf (and that it probably goes back to an older German tradition of mathematical art work). I must thank Caroline Series, Frans Oort, Paul Cohn, John Jones, Ulf Persson, David Fowler, an anonymous referee/ˌref.əˈriː/ 审稿人 and David Tranah from C.U.P. for helpful comments on the preprint/ˈpriː.prɪnt/ 预印本 version of this book, and apologise/əˈpɒl.ə.dʒaɪz/ 道歉 if on occasions I have either not been fully able to accommodate their suggestions, or preferred my own counsel.

出现在 (2.14--) 中的"眨眼环面"是Jim Eells告诉我的，他说他是从H. Hopf那里学到的（而且这可能可以追溯到更早的德国数学艺术传统）。我必须感谢Caroline Series、Frans Oort、Paul Cohn、John Jones、Ulf Persson、David Fowler、一位匿名审稿人以及剑桥大学出版社的David Tranah对本书预印本版本提出的有益意见，如果有些时候我未能充分采纳他们的建议，或更倾向于自己的判断，在此致歉。

I am grateful to Martina Jaeger for a number of corrections to the first printing, and to Isao Wakabayashi for a detailed reading, which uncovered many inaccuracies/ɪnˈæk.jʊ.rə.siz/ 不准确之处. I thank especially R.J. Chapman and Bill Bruce for pointing out the most serious error of the first printing (I avoided mention of the Hessian at the start of (7.2) by appealing to a false statement left as an exercise).

我感谢Martina Jaeger对第一版印刷的多处勘误，以及Isao Wakabayashi的仔细阅读，他发现了许多不准确之处。我要特别感谢R.J. Chapman和Bill Bruce指出了第一版印刷中最严重的错误（我在 (7.2) 开头避免提及Hessian矩阵，转而引用了一个留作习题的错误命题）。
