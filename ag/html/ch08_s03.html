8.3 Rigour, the First Wave

严格化的第一波

Following the introduction of abstract algebra by Hilbert and Emmy Noether, rigorous foundations for algebraic geometry were laid in the 1920s and 1930s by van der Waerden, Zariski and Weil (van der Waerden's contribution is often suppressed/səˈprest/ 被压制的，被隐没的, apparently because a number of mathematicians of the immediate postwar/ˈpəʊst.wɔːr/ 战后的 period, including some of the leading algebraic geometers, considered him a Nazi collaborator/kəˈlæb.ə.reɪ.tər/ 合作者，通敌者).

在Hilbert和Emmy Noether引入抽象代数之后，van der Waerden、Zariski和Weil在20世纪20年代和30年代为代数几何奠定了严格的基础（van der Waerden的贡献常常被忽略，显然是因为战后初期一些数学家，包括一些领先的代数几何学家，认为他是纳粹的合作者）。

A central plank/plæŋk/ 要点，纲领 of their program was to make algebraic geometry work over an arbitrary/ˈɑː.bɪ.trər.i/ 任意的 field. In this connection, a key foundational difficulty is that you can't just define a variety to be a point set: if you start life with a variety $V \subset \mathbb{A}^n_k$ over a given field $k$ then $V$ is not just a subset of $k^n$; you must also allow $K$-valued points of $V$ for field extensions $k \subset K$ (see (8.13, c) for a discussion). This is one reason for the notation $\mathbb{A}^n_k$, to mean the $k$-valued points of a variety $\mathbb{A}^n$ that one would like to think of as existing independently of the specified field $k$.

他们纲领的核心要点是使代数几何能在任意域上运作。在这一背景下，一个关键的基础性困难在于你不能仅仅把簇定义为一个点集：如果你从给定域 $k$ 上的簇 $V \subset \mathbb{A}^n_k$ 出发，那么 $V$ 不仅仅是 $k^n$ 的一个子集；你还必须允许 $V$ 的 $K$-值点，其中 $k \subset K$ 是域扩张（参见 (8.13, c) 的讨论）。这是采用记号 $\mathbb{A}^n_k$ 的一个原因，它表示簇 $\mathbb{A}^n$ 的 $k$-值点，而我们希望将 $\mathbb{A}^n$ 想象为独立于所指定的域 $k$ 而存在。

The necessity of allowing the ground field to change throughout the argument added enormously/ɪˈnɔː.məs.li/ 极大地 to the technical and conceptual difficulties (to say nothing of the notation). However, by around 1950, Weil's system of foundations was accepted as the norm, to the extent that traditional geometers (such as Hodge and Pedoe) felt compelled/kəmˈpeld/ 被迫的 to base their books on it, much to the detriment/ˈdet.rɪ.mənt/ 损害, I believe, of their readability.

允许基域在论证过程中不断变化的必要性，极大地增加了技术和概念上的困难（更不用说记号了）。然而，到了1950年前后，Weil的基础体系被接受为标准，以至于传统的几何学家（如Hodge和Pedoe）都感到不得不以此为基础来写书，我认为这大大损害了他们著作的可读性。
